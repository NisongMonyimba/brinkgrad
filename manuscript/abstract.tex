We present \texttt{micro\-grad}, an open-source FEniCSx implementation
of density-based topology optimisation for coupled Brinkman--convection-diffusion systems.
The implementation integrates a continuous adjoint for a multi-objective
cost functional coupling viscous dissipation and outlet concentration mismatch,
SUPG stabilisation of both forward and adjoint transport equations
following standard practice~\cite{donea2003} at
$\Pe\sim10^2$--$10^5$,
and a modular continuation schedule that reduces the gray-zone fraction
from $\grayShortRun$ to $\grayFrac$ over $\nIterPrimary$ iterations.
Gradient direction is verified by finite-difference Taylor test (slope $\approx 2.0$)
and Pearson correlation $\geq 0.80$ with finite-difference gradients;
the OC and MMA optimisers depend only on gradient sign, confirmed numerically
as validated by direct comparison against pure MMA
(Table~\ref{tab:mma_comparison}).

As a verification benchmark, the implementation achieves
RMSE~$=\rmseTopOpt$ for a linear concentration gradient
on an $80\times20$ mesh.
The linear target serves as a sanity check;
the hybrid OC/MMA schedule is validated by direct comparison
against pure MMA (RMSE~$=\rmsePureMMA$ vs.\ $\rmseShortRun$
at 600 iterations, Table~\ref{tab:mma_comparison}).
The optimisation is not mesh-convergent: finer meshes find
different local minima (RMSE~$=\rmseHiRes$--$\rmseHiResScaled$).
\texttt{micro\-grad} optimises within the Brinkman--SIMP porous-medium model;
the optimised permeability field is \emph{not} a manufacturable design.
Hard-thresholding to binary permeability increases RMSE by $\binaryGapPct\%$,
confirming that the surrogate exploits gray-zone diffusion unavailable
in physical geometries.
Users requiring manufacturable designs should apply robust projection
methods~\cite{wang2011} during optimisation.
The complete pipeline is reproducible in approximately one hour on a standard laptop
from a single Docker command and is archived with a Zenodo DOI~\cite{micrograd2025zenodo}.
